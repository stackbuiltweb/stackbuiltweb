Sofia Reyes.

I picked up my first camera at nineteen and never looked back. What started as an obsession with light — the way it curves around a face, catches the edge of a wave — became a career I can't imagine living without.

For the past ten years, I've been photographing families, couples, entrepreneurs, and spaces along Florida's Space Coast. I work out of Cocoa Beach, but I'll travel anywhere the light is good.

My style is simple: natural, warm, and honest. I don't pose people into shapes they don't fit. I find the moment — and I wait for it.

The long version.

I grew up in Miami, the daughter of a commercial fisherman and a high school art teacher. My mother painted. My father told stories. I ended up somewhere between the two — making images that try to tell stories about light.

I studied visual arts at UCF, but the real education happened after graduation — assisting wedding photographers, shooting real estate on weekends, learning to read a room and anticipate a moment before it happens.

In 2018, I moved to Cocoa Beach. Something about the quality of light here — the way the Atlantic catches the morning sun, the golden hours that seem to last forever in the fall — it changed my work completely. The color palette shifted. The pace slowed down. Everything became warmer.

Today, I split my time between portrait sessions on the beach, editorial work for regional publications, and the occasional wedding that makes me cry behind the camera. I love what I do. And I think that shows up in the work.

"The best photographs happen when people forget there's a camera in the room."
